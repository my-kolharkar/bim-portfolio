Some things I understood much more deeply after writing them out: 🔹 Why Search Sets beat Selection Sets in Navisworks Clash Detective (they're dynamic — auto-update when the model changes) 🔹 Why LOD matters for clash detection (LOD 200 coordination is almost useless — insulation clashes only appear at LOD 300+) 🔹 Why shared coordinates are non-negotiable before appending models in Navisworks Happy to share the PDF with anyone preparing for MEP BIM roles. #RevitMEP #Navisworks #BIM #SelfLearning #MEPEngineering #ClashDetection

📣 Post — Clash Detection Insight

One thing that surprised me when I first learned Navisworks Clash Detective: Running ALL elements vs. ALL elements produces thousands of results — most of them noise. The real skill is in the test setup. What I now do instead: 1️⃣ Structure vs. Mechanical Ductwork first (highest priority — beams are fixed, ducts must move) 2️⃣ Structure vs. All Pipework 3️⃣ Structure vs. Cable Trays 4️⃣ Mechanical vs. Pipework 5️⃣ Pipework vs. Cable Trays (with clearance tests) Use Search Sets for each group — not manual selections. They auto-update when the model refreshes, so re-running tests after model updates takes seconds, not hours. This is the kind of practical workflow knowledge that makes a difference in a real coordination project.
